League Overview:

  • We compete in a 10-team league consisting of 2 divisions.
  • Games are played head-to-head against a different opponent each week resulting in a win, loss, or tie based on the posted scoring system.
  • The regular season is weeks 1-13, the postseason is weeks 14-16.
  • The regular season schedule is set by the website.
  • Each team has a maximum of a 16-man roster (not counting IR)
  • Starting Lineup: 1 QB, 2 RB, 3 WR, 1 TE, 1 K, 1 D/ST
  • Bench: any combination of positions for these 7 roster spots
  • The site locks players' starting status 5 minutes prior to kickoff of that player's NFL game.
  • In the event of a tie, the tiebreaker is the single highest-scoring player on each team's bench
    • These points do not count towards the score
    • If both teams' top bench players tie, the result is a tie

Draft:

  • The draft is held as close to the season's start as possible.
  • Draft order is posted on the site. Order is reversed for all even numbered rounds (a "snake draft"), except the 2nd round.
  • All draft picks must be used, and all teams must draft a full starting team.
  • The draft lasts 16 rounds:
    • The first 2 rounds of the draft are designated as "Keeper Rounds", and does NOT SNAKE.
    • Round 3 begins the regular draft, with the #1 draft position picking first.
    • Round 4 is the first to "snake", with all even # rounds running in reverse-draft-order.

Fees & Payouts:

  • Entry fee - $50, due before the preseason draft
  • Transaction fees (regular season)- $3 per player "added", due ASAP when your team is eliminated from postseason play.
  • The cumulative league entry and transaction fees are split into 20 payouts (5% each) as follows:
    • 1 for making the playoffs (6 teams)
    • 1/2 for winning your division (2)
    • 1/2 for each: best record, points & power ranking champ (3)
    • 2 for making the Super Bowl (2)
    • 6-1/2 for winning the Super Bowl
    • 1 for winning the Toilet Bowl

Next Season:

  • Draft order is determined by reverse-order of last year's standings with the following exceptions:
    • the winner of the Toilet Bowl is awarded the #1 pick slot
    • the Super Bowl winner picks 10th, and the SB loser picks 9th.
  • We are a Keeper League:
    • The last team to declare their keepers (including changes) will be charged an additional $3
    • Each team is allowed to keep up to 2 players from their year-end roster
    • If a team keeps 1 player, that constitutes their 1st round pick
    • If a team keeps 2 players, both their 1st & 2nd round picks are used
    • Trading of Next Year's Draft Picks is NOT ALLOWED
    • Offseason trades are allowed: any player traded during the offseason is designated as a Keeper for the team who is giving up the player.
  • If any owner is unable to return the following year, the new owner takes over the existing franchise, including roster and draft slot.

Transactions:

  • Transactions (add/drop's) may be made at any time, from the time your roster is posted to the site.
  • Any add/drop requested after kickoff of the week's first game is subject to waivers.
  • Trades are allowed, and are subject to a 2 day veto process. It takes 6 objections to veto a trade.
  • The trade deadline is set on the website (typically week 11)
  • Lazy-Ass Fee/Illegal Lineup/Roster Fee - $9 is charged to any owner who starts a player during their bye week, or when a player is listed on the injury report as "out".
  • Waivers
    • Any add/drop's requested after kickoff of the week's first game until midnight Tue are subject to waivers
    • Waivers run every Tue night between midnight and 6 a.m. Wed morning
    • Players are awarded to teams in reverse-record order (with points as the tiebreaker)
    • Submit as many waiver claims as you like, but once awarded one player, you move to the end of the line for your next waiver claim
    • Any add/drop's requested after kickoff of the week's first game until midnight Tue are subject to waivers

Injured Reserve:

  • The site allows players to go on IR when they are on IR for their NFL team. This is beneficial for players you may want to keep next year. The site decides when and how this works.

Postseason:

  • Home-field Advantage
    • 3 points are awarded to the higher cede in weeks 14 & 15
    • There is no bonus during week 16
  • Transaction fees (postseason)
    • $9 per player "added", due ASAP when your team is eliminated from postseason play
    • All fees collected for postseason transactions go to the Super Bowl teams and the Toilet Bowl winner.
  • Postseason runs weeks 14-16
    • We give each team a league ranking, or cede, based on regular season record, from 1-10 (see Tiebreakers below)
    • The 'regular season champ' earns the #1 cede and the other division winner is #2
    • Cedes 1-6 will go on to the 'Super Bowl Playoffs'
    • Cede #'s 7-10 will enter the 'Toilet Bowl Tournament'
  • Tiebreakers
    1. Head-to-head (1a *For Division Champ Only* Division Record)
    2. Points scored
    3. Power ranking
    4. Total points (including backups)
    5. Points Against
    6. Points scored in final week
    7. Pts scored in previous wk, etc...
